## Why Do You Need a Retainer After Orthodontic Treatment?

When your braces come off or your aligners are no longer part of your daily routine, it's tempting to think your orthodontic journey is over. However, your teeth are still not fully settled in their new positions. The tissues, ligaments, and bones that hold your teeth in place need time to stabilize. Without the consistent use of a retainer, teeth will naturally try to move back to their original positions—this process is called relapse.

So, if you're wondering "how important are retainers," know this: your retainer is the key to locking in the smile you've worked so hard to achieve. Think of retainers as your final step of treatment—the step that transforms temporary results into lifelong ones.

At Benkovich Orthodontics, we see orthodontics as an investment in your confidence and oral health. Retainers protect that investment, ensuring your teeth stay straight and your smile stays radiant.

## How Important Are Retainers for Lifelong Alignment?

Teeth aren't static; they're always shifting slowly throughout your life due to natural factors like chewing, changes in your jaw, or even the effects of aging. That means even people who've never had braces experience tooth movement over time. If you don't wear your retainer consistently, your teeth can shift out of alignment.

Still questioning "how important are retainers"? Think about the potential long-term consequences of not wearing one:

- **Crowded teeth:** Misaligned teeth make it harder to brush and floss effectively, increasing the risks of cavities and gum disease.
- **Bite issues:** Shifting teeth can lead to an improper bite, which might cause jaw pain or uneven wear on your teeth.
- **Orthodontic retreatment:** Neglecting a retainer could mean needing braces or aligners again in the future, costing you both time and money.

Retainers aren't just for aesthetics—they're essential for a healthy bite and dental function in the long term. At Benkovich Orthodontics, we emphasize that consistent retainer wear is the easiest way to keep your smile in perfect shape.

## What Types of Retainers Are Available?

At Benkovich Orthodontics, we understand that every patient is unique, which is why we offer different types of [retainers](/retainers/) to suit your needs. Choosing the right retainer is important for maintaining consistency and making retainer wear part of your routine.

### Removable Retainers

Removable retainers are popular because of their convenience. These retainers come in two forms:

- **Clear plastic retainers (Essix):** These are nearly invisible and fit snugly over your teeth. They're a great option for patients who value aesthetics and simplicity.
- **Traditional acrylic retainers (Hawley):** Made from durable acrylic with a metal wire, these are slightly more noticeable but very durable and adjustable.

Removable retainers are easy to take out while eating or cleaning, but they require dedication. Forgetting to wear them—even for a short period—can lead to shifting teeth.

### Fixed Retainers

Fixed retainers are thin, discreet wires bonded to the back of your teeth and remain in place 24/7. These are an excellent choice for patients prone to forgetting to wear their removable retainers or those at higher risk of relapse.

Although fixed retainers are "set it and forget it" devices, they do require regular cleaning to ensure food particles don't accumulate around the wire.

At Benkovich Orthodontics, we'll recommend the retainer that best suits your lifestyle, ensuring your long-term smile stability.

## How to Properly Care for Your Retainer

Now that you know how important are retainers, it's time to make sure they stay in the best possible shape. Proper care of your retainer ensures it remains effective and lasts as long as possible.

- **Clean it daily:** For removable retainers, gently clean them with a toothbrush and lukewarm water every day. Avoid using hot water, as it can warp the material.
- **Store it safely:** Always place your removable retainer in its case when you're not wearing it to avoid losing or damaging it.
- **Maintain fixed retainers:** Brush carefully around the wire and use floss threaders or water flossers to keep the area clean.
- **Inspect for wear and tear:** Over time, retainers can show signs of damage or lose their perfect fit. Visit Benkovich Orthodontics if you notice cracks, wear, or poor fit—don't wait until it's too late.
- **Bring it to check-ups:** Show your retainer to your orthodontist at regular appointments to confirm it's still in good shape and working effectively.

A little extra effort goes a long way toward ensuring your retainer continues to do its job effectively.
