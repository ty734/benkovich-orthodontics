When deciding to straighten your teeth using Invisalign, one key question comes to mind for almost everyone: how long does Invisalign take? If you're hoping to achieve a beautiful smile without the hassle of traditional braces, Invisalign offers an effective, convenient solution. But how long will you have to commit to wearing these clear aligners? In this blog, we'll explore the average timeline for Invisalign treatment and discuss what factors can influence the duration of your plan.

## Introduction to Invisalign

Invisalign is a modern orthodontic treatment that uses a series of clear aligner trays to gradually move and straighten teeth. Like traditional braces, its purpose is to correct alignment issues such as crowding, gaps, overbites, underbites, and crossbites. What sets Invisalign apart is its almost invisible design, allowing patients the discretion to correct their smile without the metal brackets and wires associated with traditional braces.

With Invisalign, you'll wear each set of trays for about 1 or 2 weeks before moving to the next set in the series. The number of aligners you'll need — and consequently how long does Invisalign take — will depend on how much your teeth need to move and the complexity of your orthodontic issues.

## How Long Does Invisalign Take on Average?

While the specific timeline can vary, most people who undergo Invisalign treatment are finished between 12 to 18 months. However, in some cases, treatment may be as short as 6 months for minor corrections or up to 24 months or longer for more complicated dental issues.

## Invisalign Treatment Timeline by Case Type

1. **Minor Dental Adjustments (6–12 Months)** If you're only dealing with small gaps, minor crowding, or slight adjustments to crooked teeth, how long does Invisalign take? For these simpler cases, treatment can be completed in as little as 6–12 months. In some instances, patients may notice visible improvements in their smile in the first 3 months or so.
2. **Moderate Dental Adjustments (12–18 Months)** For patients with moderate tooth misalignment or spacing issues, Invisalign usually requires about 12 to 18 months of treatment. This is the most common timeline, as moderate cases involve several teeth needing to shift to new positions. In these instances, small changes accumulate slowly but continue gradually throughout the treatment.
3. **Severe or Complex Cases (18–24+ Months)** Patients with more complicated dental issues like significant overcrowding, large gaps, or bite problems generally require longer treatment. Severe alignment corrections can range from 18 months to over 2 years. Even though these cases take longer, the results are well worth it, as Invisalign can handle challenging dental conditions that require precision and significant adjustments.

## Why Does Invisalign Treatment Time Vary?

So, how long does Invisalign take, and why does it vary so much from one patient to another? Several factors contribute to the length of your treatment. Let's break down the main factors:

### 1. Complexity of Your Dental Issues

The more complex your orthodontic issues, the longer your Invisalign treatment will take. For instance, having mild crowding is quicker to correct than a deep overbite. Additionally, straightening several teeth or working on both dental arches will require more time than correcting issues related to only one set (either upper or lower teeth).

### 2. Patient Compliance

How long does Invisalign take if you don't wear your aligners regularly? The alignment process requires patients to wear their aligners for at least 22 hours a day. While Invisalign offers the flexibility of removing trays when eating, drinking, or brushing teeth, wearing them less than 22 hours per day will lengthen treatment time and delay results.

### 3. Progress Monitoring and Aligners Changes

Invisalign treatment works by swapping out aligners every 1 to 2 weeks. Your orthodontist will give you a schedule to follow, and staying on track is essential. Missing orthodontic check-ups or failing to switch aligners regularly can impact how long Invisalign takes overall. Keep up with the prescribed timeline to maintain progress and stay on track!

### 4. Age

Invisalign works great for both teens and adults alike. However, teeth tend to move more slowly in adults, as their jaws have fully developed. Younger patients (such as teens) generally experience slightly quicker results because their teeth shift more easily with orthodontic treatments.

## Steps You Can Take to Speed Up the Process

While some factors, like the complexity of your dental issues or age, are outside your control, there are certainly steps you can take to ensure your Invisalign treatment proceeds as quickly as possible. Want to speed up how long does Invisalign take? Here are a few tips to stay on track:

1. **Wear Your Aligners as Recommended:** The most important way to expedite your treatment is to wear your aligners for the required 22 hours per day. You should only remove them when eating, drinking (anything besides water), and for oral hygiene. Wearing them for fewer hours means less progress!
2. **Stick to Your Aligner-Swap Schedule:** Be diligent about switching to the next set of aligners on time. [Aligners](/invisalign-info/invisalign-for-adults/) are generally changed every one to two weeks as your teeth shift according to plan. Delayed swaps equal delayed progress.
3. **Attend Checkups:** Regular check-ins with your orthodontist are critical for staying on course. Missing appointments could mean that your orthodontist doesn't catch potential problems early on, which could ultimately affect your progress.
4. **Keep Your Aligners Clean:** Dirty or damaged aligners can affect their fit, and poorly fitting aligners slow down tooth movement. Make sure to clean your aligners regularly and follow your orthodontist's care instructions.
5. **Consider Using Aligner Chewies:** Aligner chewies are small, soft devices you chew on to ensure your aligners fit snugly against your teeth. By using them a few times a day, you can help seat your aligners more effectively, which ensures your teeth are being moved as expected.

## How Does Invisalign Compare to Traditional Braces?

Many patients ask how long does Invisalign take compared to traditional braces. For simple or moderate cases, Invisalign treatment is often quicker than traditional braces. While traditional metal or ceramic braces treatments can take anywhere between 18 months and 3 years, Invisalign averages around 12 to 18 months.

However, for severe cases (like extremely complex bite issues), the timelines for both treatment options may be similar. That said, Invisalign offers more comfort, flexibility, and aesthetic benefits during treatment.
