Most people associate orthodontic care with Invisalign or usual braces. There is a more modern procedure, Accelerated Osteogenic Orthodontics (AOO), also known as Wilckodontics. It assures quicker and more effective results. This creative approach marries orthodontic therapy with small surgery to greatly accelerate tooth motion.

Though it has many advantages, before determining if it is the best option for you, it is important to know both the benefits and possible downsides. In this comprehensive blog guide, we will consider the advantages and disadvantages of Wilckodontics to enable you to come to an informed decision.

## What is Wilckodontics?

If you want to know [**what is Wilckodontics**](/treatment-options/wilckodontics/) then it is a specialized orthodontic therapy. It combines traditional Invisalign or conventional braces via a small surgery called Corticotomy. The operation calls for carefully slicing the bone around the teeth, encouraging the bone's natural healing mechanism, and thereby enabling the teeth to move more freely. This allows for an orthodontic therapy that is speedier and more effective than traditional techniques.

It aims mainly to shorten treatment times, increase bone density, and advance general orthodontic outcomes. Although quite advantageous, one should consider the benefits and drawbacks of this type of therapy.

## Pros of Wilckodontics

### 1. Significantly Faster Treatment Time

One of the biggest advantages of Wilckodontics is its ability to speed up orthodontic treatment. Traditional braces can take 18 months to 3 years to straighten teeth, while it can achieve the same results in as little as 3 to 8 months. This makes it an excellent choice for those who want a straighter smile quickly.

### 2. More Effective Tooth Movement

The surgical procedure involved in Wilckodontics softens the bone structure around the teeth, making it easier for them to move into their ideal position. This allows orthodontists to correct complex misalignments more efficiently and with greater precision.

### 3. Long-Lasting and Stable Results

Because Wilckodontics promotes bone remodeling, the new bone structure provides better support for the teeth, reducing the chances of teeth shifting back after treatment. This enhances the long-term stability of your orthodontic results.

### 4. Suitable for a Wide Range of Cases

**Wilckodontics** is effective for treating moderate to severe malocclusions, including crowding, overbites, underbites, and crossbites. It is especially useful for adults who may have slower tooth movement due to denser jawbone structure.

### 5. Reduced Risk of Root Resorption

Traditional orthodontic treatments can sometimes lead to root resorption, where the roots of the teeth shrink due to prolonged pressure. Since Wilckodontics accelerates tooth movement, the time spent wearing braces is reduced, which lowers the risk of root resorption.

### 6. Improved Gum and Bone Health

The surgical process stimulates bone regeneration and remodeling, which can enhance overall gum and bone health. This makes it a beneficial option for patients with weaker bone structures or those at risk of periodontal issues.

## Cons of Wilckodontics

### 1. Requires a Surgical Procedure

Unlike traditional braces or Invisalign, it involves minor oral surgery. Although the surgery is minimally invasive, it still requires local anesthesia and a short recovery period. Some patients may experience swelling, mild discomfort, or bleeding after the procedure.

### 2. Higher Cost Compared to Traditional Braces

[**Wilckodontics**](/treatment-options/wilckodontics/) is more expensive than conventional orthodontic treatments due to the surgical component and the specialized expertise required. The cost can vary depending on the complexity of the case and the dental provider. Insurance may not fully cover this procedure, so it's important to check with your provider before committing to treatment.

### 3. Potential for Temporary Discomfort

Patients undergoing Wilckodontics may experience mild to moderate pain or sensitivity following the surgical procedure. This discomfort is usually temporary and can be managed with over-the-counter pain medication, but it's something to consider if you have a low pain tolerance.

### 4. Not Suitable for Everyone

While it is highly effective, it may not be suitable for all patients. Individuals with severe periodontal disease, certain medical conditions, or weak jawbone structure may not be good candidates. A thorough evaluation by an orthodontist is necessary to determine eligibility.

### 5. Requires a Specialist

Not all orthodontists are trained in Wilckodontics. Since this procedure requires both an orthodontist and a periodontist or oral surgeon, you may need to visit a specialized clinic or a team of professionals who are experienced in this technique. Finding a qualified provider can sometimes be challenging.

### 6. Possibility of Minor Surgical Risks

As with any surgical procedure, there are minor risks, such as infection, swelling, or gum sensitivity. However, these risks are rare when performed by an experienced specialist and can be managed with proper post-operative care.
